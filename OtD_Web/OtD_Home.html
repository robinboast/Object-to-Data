Course Description

All humanities disciplines are confronted with more and more digital material. This digital material can be studied as individual objects or descriptions of events, but from a digital perspective they can also be considered as data points. Digital methods allow researchers to study relations between objects form a different perspective and on a larger scale.

How can humanities researchers use digital data to support their research? What digital tools are at their disposal and how can these tools provide new perspectives and research questions? This course looks at digital data sources and tools that are relevant to the humanities from a programmatic perspective.

Course Goals

After completing this course the student is able to:

  1. explain how a shift in perspective on humanities material from objects as objects to objects as data points affects humanities research;
  2. explain the relevance of data-oriented research for humanities disciplines;
  3. describe and apply digital and programmatic concepts for working with humanities data;
  4. create a methodologically sound data-oriented humanities research proposal;
  5. structure and conduct digital research around a research question with humanities materials

Grading

Students build up a portfolio of scripts and documentation that together form a coherent data-driven research project.

  • Each student writes a short proposal for a collaborative research project for a given data set. The proposal will be graded.
  • Students form groups and choose one of their proposals as group project. Each group creates a project portfolio, which will be graded twice: in week 5 and in the exam week (week 8).
  • The final grade will be based on a weighted average:
    • project proposal - 20%
    • project portfolio, first version - 20%
    • project portfolio, final version - 60%
  • Students may update and improve on any aspect of their portfolios between weeks 5 and 8.
